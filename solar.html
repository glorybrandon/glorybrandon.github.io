Solar System

The Solar System is the gravitationally bound system of the Sun and the objects that orbit it, either directly or indirectly.

Of the objects that orbit the Sun directly, the largest are the eight planets, with the remainder being smaller objects, the dwarf planets and small Solar System bodies.The Solar System formed 4.6 billion years ago from the gravitational collapse of a giant interstellar molecular cloud.

Natural Satellite

A natural satellite, or moon, is, in the most common usage, an astronomical body that orbits a planet or minor planet (or sometimes another small Solar System body). In the Solar System, there are six planetary satellite systems containing 205 known natural satellites.
